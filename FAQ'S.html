Frequently Asked Questions About Tree Plantation

1. What is the current state of internet usage in rural India?

Rural India had more than 425 million internet users as of December 2022, a whopping 44% more than urban India, which had 295 million users. The active internet user base aged 12 years and older had grown more than 20% year-on-year to reach 595 million in 2022.

2. How does internet usage in rural areas compare to urban areas?

Worldwide, 82% of urban dwellers are using the internet in 2022, which is 1.8 times higher than the percentage of internet users in rural areas. However, the urban-rural gap has been narrowing over the last three years as rural areas gradually catch up.

3. What are the main barriers to internet adoption in rural India?

The primary deterrents to internet adoption in rural areas are difficulty in understanding the internet and lack of awareness. Language barriers and lack of infrastructure also continue to limit overall internet penetration despite growing mobile access.

4. How is internet access being provided in rural villages?

Projects like the one in Melur, India, have set up internet kiosks in centrally located places designated as shops to minimize the effect of caste on access. The model is based on the paid usage of internet by villagers, and kiosk operators are entrepreneurs from the village.

5. What are the impacts of increased internet access on rural communities?

Increased internet access has led to a steady increase in interactions between communities and local government officials due to the introduction of e-government services. It has also caused a paradigm shift in the attitudes of villagers, who are now realizing the opportunities available to them and seeking help from distant sources like hospitals.

6. What are the future prospects for internet usage in rural India?

The report titled 'India Internet Report 2023' mentions that nearly half of rural India was on the internet, with strong growth of 30%, and more headroom for growth in the future. Future growth is expected to come from increased vernacular content and affordable mobile access that allows more rural users to experience the online world.
